Dammit cross origin!

If you don't know what CORS is, it is dumb and stupid a system put in place for a genine reason. When the web got the <img> tag, it allowed pulling subresources from a different origin, as you, cookies and all; and it didn't stop there.

Tags like <script>, <video>, <audio>, and <iframe> were added too. each letting you get data as the browser. This would let you to go to webpages like, say, the webpage to delete your Roblox account. This was bad so they put in a way to only share what you want, Cross Origin Resource Sharing (CORS). You can read more here.

sufficed to say, theres nothing more un-trustworthy than a file on your PC trying to access another file on your PC. Thats why you want to localhost. The problem? Not wanting to use Node. Why? In this situation it's not just stubbornness, but also that I programmed a lot on my school provided laptop that doesn't even allow the debug console, let alone Node.JS. The only way to really do anything was through VSCode... and thats when I got an idea! And, would you know, one quick Google search later...
